Thai Basil Chicken

This two serving, 100% authentic Thai Basil Chicken dish is sure to delight, right up to the point it melts your face off. Most of the ingredients found here should be readily available in your home garden but, in the immortal words of Ina Garten, "store bought is fine."


Ingredients:

  • 2 Boneless Chicken Thighs
  • 2 Farm-fresh Eggs
  • 8 Cloves of Garlic
  • 5 Thai Chilies
  • 2 Cups Jasmine Rice
  • 2 Handfuls of Thai Holy Basil
  • 3 Tbsp Oil for Frying
  • 2 Tsp of Oyster Sauce
  • 1 Tsp of Soy Sauce
  • 1 Tsp of Sugar

Steps:

  1. Start cooking your Jasmine Rice. To ensure all the food is done around the same time, wait ~20 minutes before staring step #2.
  2. Add 2 tbsp of oil to your wok/pan and bring to high heat.
  3. Flash-fry your eggs. Set aside on a paper towel or oven rack.
  4. Cut your chicken into small bite-sized pieces. Rinse, peel, & mince your garlic / chilies and combine in one bowl. Mix your oyster sauce, soy sauce, and sugar in another small bowl. Pluck your thai basil leave from the stems and set aside.
  5. Still on high-heat, add the rest of your oil (1 tbsp) to your wok/pan.
  6. Add in your chilies & garlic, stir-frying for 30 seconds.
  7. Add in chicken. Ensure fully cooked to 165°.
  8. Once chicken is at least 165°, add your sauce & sugar and mix for another 30 seconds.
  9. Lastly, toss in your basil. It is very important that you wait until the chicken is fully cooked to add the basil. You want to cook here for 5-15 seconds, enough to make the basil soft and shrunk down, being careful not to over-wilt or burn it.
  10. Once your basil has "cooked-in" perfectly, immediately remove from heat.
  11. Serve your Thai Basil Chicken over a bed of Jasmine Rice, topped with one (or more!) of your flash-fried bubble eggs.
  12. Enjoy!
